Bringing Roadside Rendering to Reality:

Advancing the Conventional NeRF Pipeline for Photorealistic 3D View Synthesis in Autonomous Driving

Abstract

Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) have emerged as a prominent research area within the last three years due to demonstrating significant potential in 3D scene representation with high-fidelity novel view synthesis. Its rapid growth has extended into the simulation field of Autonomous Driving (AD). Studies have explored NeRF's potential in modeling roadside environments where recent methods have utilized NeRFs for reconstructing dynamic driving scenarios for AD systems. As a recent and emerging topic, NeRFs for AD systems have many unexplored areas that are crucial for developing safety-critical and robust AD systems to ensure smooth everyday driving to edge-case situations.

Addressing the gaps in NeRF-based AD systems, the CARLA Simulator is utilized to create the NeRF-CARLA Dynamic (NCD) dataset - a customizable synthetic dataset to design diverse and challenging driving scenarios. Integrating the dataset into a dynamic NeRF framework, the study performs compositional rendering of background and foreground to reconstruct complex roadside scenes with moving objects. To enhance the NeRF view synthesis for photorealistic outcomes, an integrated novel module 'Zip-LiX-SR' is proposed that includes a three-stage enhancement process: enhancing low-frequency learning through an extension of the Zip-NeRF model, reducing visible artifacts through an inter-viewpoint mixer of neighboring views, and integrating an advanced Super-Resolution (SR) technique to enrich high-frequency details. In addition to conventional objective metric-based evaluations, this study emphasizes visual perception through subjective assessments, supplemented by a detailed statistical analysis. The proposed module has outperformed the rest of the modules in both training trajectories as well as from rendering multiple novel viewpoints. Additional cases are also explored by utilizing the diverse NCD dataset such as novel viewpoint rendering of road contents, long driving routes, roadside scenes at traffic intersections, and congested traffic scenarios with numerous moving vehicles. The applicability of the proposed module to reconstruct real-world scenarios is also tested.

Therefore, the study addresses AD challenges by introducing a valuable synthetic driving dataset as well as proposing an effective approach to produce high-fidelity and photorealistic dynamic scene reconstruction outputs.
